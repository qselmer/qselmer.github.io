## Purpose

Pelagytics is a developing analytical platform concept intended to connect fisheries, biological, environmental, and spatial information through reproducible workflows, interpretable indicators, and decision-support products.

## Scientific problem

Fisheries analyses are often distributed across disconnected scripts, spreadsheets, databases, figures, and reports. This fragmentation makes it difficult to update results consistently, trace assumptions, compare indicators, and communicate uncertainty.

## Intended scope

- Standardized data intake and quality-control workflows.
- Reproducible calculation of biological, fisheries, and ecosystem indicators.
- Spatio-temporal summaries of populations and fishing activity.
- Model diagnostics and uncertainty communication.
- Automated scientific figures, tables, and reporting products.
- Traceable links among data, models, indicators, and management questions.

## Initial use cases

- Small-pelagic stock monitoring.
- Fleet and fishing-effort characterization.
- Juvenile-incidence and selectivity diagnostics.
- Environmental context for stock and fishery indicators.
- Multivariate stock-state and ecosystem summaries.

## Development status

**Concept and prototype development.** Pelagytics is not currently released as a public operational platform. Its architecture, governance, licensing, data-access model, and minimum viable product still require formal definition and testing.

## Design principles

- Scientific transparency before automation.
- Explicit assumptions and reproducible calculations.
- Modular components rather than a closed black box.
- Clear separation of source data, derived products, models, and decisions.
- Appropriate treatment of restricted or confidential fisheries information.
