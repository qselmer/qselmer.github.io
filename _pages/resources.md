This section brings together reproducible scientific tools, analytical workflows, and marine data resources used in quantitative ecology, fisheries science, stock assessment, environmental analysis, and spatio-temporal modelling.

## Software

Scientific packages and analytical frameworks developed to make marine and fisheries analyses more reproducible, transparent, and reusable.

Current records include:

- [`seasignals`](/software/seasignals/), for representing climate phase, magnitude, persistence, and transitions.
- [`oceancube`](/software/oceancube/), for harmonizing multidimensional marine environmental data.
- [Pelagytics](/software/pelagytics/), a developing concept for integrated fisheries analytics and decision support.

[Explore the software catalogue](/software/)

## Data

A curated catalogue of open, registered-access, and research-grade biological, fisheries, acoustic, oceanographic, satellite, climate, and geospatial data resources.

The catalogue distinguishes source data from derived products and records access conditions, scale, format, provider, and intended analytical use.

[Explore data resources](/data/)

## Reproducible workflows

Software and data are connected through project-specific workflows for acquisition, quality control, harmonization, modelling, visualization, and reporting. The [Reproducible marine data systems](/projects/reproducible-marine-data-systems/) project documents this broader analytical architecture.

## Access and licensing

Not all fisheries or biological datasets can be distributed publicly. Restricted data will be represented through metadata, processing descriptions, synthetic examples, or reproducible code that does not disclose confidential records. Public software releases will state their version, license, citation, and repository explicitly.
