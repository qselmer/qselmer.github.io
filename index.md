<h1 class="marine-title">Quantitative marine ecology for fisheries and ecosystem assessment</h1>

<p class="marine-subtitle">
I am a marine quantitative ecologist at IMARPE, Peru, working at the interface of fisheries science, stock assessment, spatio-temporal modelling, and reproducible scientific computing.
</p>

<p class="marine-subtitle">
My work translates complex marine ecosystem dynamics into quantitative evidence for monitoring, assessment, and decision-making in fisheries and marine resource management.
</p>

## Core areas of work
{: .section-title}

<div class="card-grid">

<div class="info-card">
<h3>Stock assessment</h3>
<p>Population dynamics, biomass, recruitment, mortality, reference points, and uncertainty-aware assessment.</p>
</div>

<div class="info-card">
<h3>Spatio-temporal ecology</h3>
<p>Species distributions, habitat use, spatial shifts, environmental gradients, and ecosystem variability.</p>
</div>

<div class="info-card">
<h3>Marine ecosystem indicators</h3>
<p>Indicators of stock state, biological condition, spatial structure, productivity, and pressure–response relationships.</p>
</div>

<div class="info-card">
<h3>Scientific computing</h3>
<p>Reproducible workflows in R, Python, Julia, GitHub, and open scientific tools for fisheries research.</p>
</div>

</div>
