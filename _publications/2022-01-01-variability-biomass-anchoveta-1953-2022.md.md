## Overview

This conference contribution examines variability in the biomass of the north-central Peruvian anchovy stock from interannual to multi-decadal scales. The analysis is intended to distinguish short-term fluctuations from longer-term structural changes within the Humboldt Current System.

## Main contribution

- Characterization of biomass variability across multiple temporal scales.
- Separation of short-term fluctuations from persistent changes in stock magnitude.
- Interpretation of long-term anchovy dynamics within an eastern boundary upwelling system.
- Implications for monitoring, assessment, and management under strong environmental variability.
